Analytics Platform Implementation
Rising licensing and maintenance costs of our existing DBMS platform necessitated moving to a more cost effective solution. I proposed we investigate different data storage/query solutions for our analysis project. My proposal resulted in ...my leading the effort to identify system requirements and creating a team to evaluate alternatives for a new data storage platform that would host 100TB of data and support analysis through performant querying. The approach I took involved the following steps:
  • Define requirements and constraints to include (money, size, power, cooling, ability to query, low side/high side--loading fast on H/S)
  • Define evaluation methods for systems and write an AoA -- what are the tactical advantages to an approach and what are the strategic advantages
  • Identify the challenges the future is likely to to bring
  • Building a team to execute the system evaluations
  • Come up with and execute a plan that proved efficient data loads and data extract
  • Choose a system that met constraints, then work with local resources to implement the system

Project Planning
I led a team with the charter to create a web-based analytical platform supporting remote sites across the US. I knew a linear waterfall methodology wouldn't yield a relevant solution. I sought a better approach and Iterative development (SCRUM) looked like a great alternative...What was the approach Who were the actors -- DoD was a funding source along with DOE. We had a Product Owner -- who wasn't really able to articulate what the product should be. A project manager who helped track budgets, prepare status reports, and take care of project administration, developers, technical writer, tester, project admin, and PI. I was basically charged with identifying the requirements, direction, and tasks we were taking (I was acting as the surrogate product owner). I recruited the team members at PNNL and identified the approach for running the project (SCRUM) which we took the team, including the product owner, to training in Seattle.
